⚙️ Cost & Ridership Settings

Editable estimates used to compute the stats shown when hovering a route line. Defaults are derived from real MTA/NYCT data — adjust to match your scenario.
📊 Data sources (MTA 2024)
NYCT bus: ~1.29M weekday boardings / ~300 routes → ~4,300/route/day system avg.
That avg is dragged down by low-ridership outer-borough routes. Dense Bronx/Brooklyn/Queens locals (e.g. Bx39 White Plains Rd ~10K/day, ~80 stops) work out to ~7–8/stop/hr — hence the default of 8.
High-ridership SBS (Bx41 ~22K/day, Bx12 ~25K/day): fewer stops, so ~15–25/stop/hr.
Operating cost: MTA ~$250/bus-revenue-hr; ~$30/vehicle-mile (highest per-mile cost of any US city bus system).
Speed: NYC local avg 7–9 mph incl. stops & traffic; SBS ~10–12 mph.
Low-traffic outer-borough local ~3–5 · Dense Bronx/Bklyn/Queens local ~7–10 · High-ridership SBS corridor ~15–25
